# D-Heart Pro — Case Study

**URL:** https://shakib.design/projects/d-heart

## Summary

End-to-end UX research and redesign of the D-Heart portable ECG ecosystem (device + companion app) for nurses in nursing-home settings. Academic project at Politecnico di Milano, in partnership with D-Heart S.r.l. and Design Group Italia. Four months, team of four.

**Shakib's role:** UX Researcher & Design System Lead.

---

## Problem

D-Heart's existing app had an 80% error rate on the device-placement task — the most critical step in ECG measurement. Nurses in nursing homes were struggling to correctly position the ECG clips on patients, and the app's UI gave no meaningful guidance. The device also had a single LED providing ambiguous feedback conflating ECG signal, battery, and connectivity states.

---

## Research

- **Heuristic evaluation** (Nielsen's 10) on both Android and iOS versions
- **Usability test video review** — watched recorded sessions to extract hesitation patterns, task failures, and behavioural pain points beyond quantitative KPI data
- **KPI-based usability testing** across professional users (nurses) and standard users — measuring error rates, conversion rates, and time-on-task
- **Open card sorting** with 63 participants (40 online + 13 in-person) to validate information architecture
- **Tree testing** with 44 healthcare professionals in Italian, English, and Turkish — 9 tasks
- **SEQ** (Single Ease Question), **UEQ** (User Experience Questionnaire, 6 dimensions), **PrEmo** (Desmet's emotional response tool — dominant emotions: admiration, hope, fascination)

---

## Design

**App redesign:** Rebuilt the onboarding and placement guidance flow with step-by-step animated instructions, contextual feedback, and clear state indicators. Simplified the navigation architecture based on card sorting and tree testing findings.

**Design system:** Built from scratch — typography scale, colour palette, grid system (2- and 3-column), corner-radius logic, full component library (inputs, buttons, navigation, text fields).

**Device redesign:** Redesigned ECG clips for patient comfort — the previous design caused pain during use. Added a secondary status LED to separate ECG signal state from connectivity and battery — eliminating the single-LED ambiguity.

## Outcomes

- Baseline device-placement error rate: **80%** → addressed through redesigned guidance flow
- Tree testing post-redesign: **71% success rate, 80% directness** across 9 tasks
- Final SEQ: **6.25/7** across 6 testers
- PrEmo dominant emotions: admiration, hope, fascination
