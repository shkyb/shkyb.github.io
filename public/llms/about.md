# Shakib Alipour — About

## Identity

Interaction designer working at the seam between hardware and software. Before starting my MSc at Politecnico di Milano in 2023, I spent five years as a product designer at Farassoo Tech shipping mass-produced consumer electronics and their companion apps, and three years earlier as a research assistant at CAST (University of Tehran) contributing to the SURENA IV and SURENA Mini humanoid robots. That combination — industrial design, UX research, design systems, and human-robot interaction — shapes how I approach every project: physical form and digital interface as one system, designed for real users under real constraints.

## Experience

**Rehabilia Technologies, Lecco, Italy — Interaction Design Intern** (Sep–Oct 2025)
Prototyped Phil, the social-robot evolution of PhiCube, for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder. Ran two rounds of Wizard-of-Oz user studies with 8 autistic children aged 4–9 at Fabula Clinic in Milan. Key insight: Phil as a mediating third presence between therapist and child, not an autonomous agent. Supervisor: Dr. Matteo Malosio (CEO).

**Farassoo Tech, Tehran, Iran — Product Designer** (Nov 2018 – Aug 2023, ~5 years)
End-to-end product design across hardware and digital: industrial design of mass-produced consumer electronics (SOLIDWORKS Class A/B surfaces), UX/UI for mobile apps and websites across fintech, fitness, health, and crypto. Produced photorealistic static and video renderings in KeyShot for marketing and client presentations. Created promotional videos in Adobe Premiere Pro. Built the Asemoun Design System from scratch (RTL/Persian, web + Android POS). Advocated for and embedded design thinking in a developer-led environment. Close daily collaboration with electronic engineers, mechanical engineers, and firmware developers.

**CAST, University of Tehran — Research Assistant** (Nov 2015 – May 2018)
Contributed to the design and fabrication of SURENA IV and SURENA Mini humanoid robots (IEEE Spectrum featured). Designed chassis for manufacturing, contributed to a monocoque solution that reduced robot weight, coordinated design–engineering–AI team communication.
