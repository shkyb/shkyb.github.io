# ITA Airways — Case Study

**URL:** https://shakib.design/projects/ita-airways

## Summary

Ergonomics-driven usability audit and redesign of the ITA Airways booking platform — Italy's flagship carrier. Focus on the three most painful flows: flight search, benefits selection, and offers discovery. Academic project at Politecnico di Milano, course: Ergonomics Applied to the Design of Usable Web Pages and Apps. One semester, team of four.

**Shakib's role:** UX Designer & Design System Lead.

---

## Problem

ITA Airways required **29 clicks across 3 pages** to complete a basic flight check — compared to Ryanair's 14 clicks across 5 pages. Connected booking decisions (flight selection, seat, baggage, extras) were treated as separate, unrelated tasks, forcing users to repeatedly re-enter context and navigate backwards. The offers section was buried and the benefits system was opaque.

---

## Research

- **Heuristic evaluation** — Nielsen's 10 heuristics applied across the full booking flow
- **Competitor benchmarking** — Ryanair, Qatar Airways, Trenitalia, Kayak. Click-count and page-count analysis as an objective argument for redesign.
- **4 personas + empathy maps** — covering leisure travellers, frequent business travellers, families, and accessibility-needs users
- **Task matrix** — mapping all booking subtasks against user type, frequency, and pain severity
- **Usability testing** — 5 users, task-based sessions with SEQ scoring per task

---

## Design

**Design system:** Built on top of Moon (ITA's existing component library) — adapted tokens to ITA's brand, extended components for bespoke patterns: price-comparison calendar, passenger-scoped baggage selector, offers map. Type: Lato.

**Accessibility:** Every text/background combination tested against WCAG AA and AAA, and against APCA contrast standards. Colour-blindness simulations run for tritanopia, deuteranopia, achromatopsia, and tritanomaly. Dot-pattern secondary signals added to the price calendar for colour-blind users.

**IA redesign:** Flattened the booking flow from 3 separate pages to a continuous guided sequence. Connected flight, seat, and baggage selection into one coherent flow with persistent context.

---

## Outcomes

- Reduced critical path from 29 clicks to a significantly flatter task sequence
- Full WCAG AA/AAA compliance on redesigned screens
- 4-condition colour-blindness coverage
- Task success rate improvement measured via 5-user usability test (SEQ per task)
